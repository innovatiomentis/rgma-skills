# RGMA - Ad Concepts, Scripts, Captions, and Launch Content

Rio Grande Marketing Agency helps local businesses in Las Cruces, Southern New Mexico, and nearby markets build a cleaner online presence through websites, booking sites, e-commerce, logo design, SEO, and social media management.

This skill turns an offering, customer pain point, real client launch, logo launch, or vague RGMA promo request into specific ad concepts, timed presenter scripts, post-production overlay ideas, captions, and production handoff notes.

This skill does **not** write final image or video generation prompts. After the concept, script, scene, and production notes are clear, hand off to `chatgpt-image-2` for first frames and `seedance-2` for video prompts.

## Default output

Return **2 to 3 concepts max** unless the user asks for more.

Each concept must sell **one offer to one specific owner in one pain moment with one promise and one CTA**.

English is the default. Light English and Spanish code-switching is allowed when it fits Las Cruces, Southern New Mexico, border-region, Hispanic-owned business, restaurant, beauty, family service, retail, trades, or community business context.

Tone: direct, local, helpful, confident, practical. Never corporate, never desperate, never fake, never overproduced.

## The secret key

The best RGMA ad is not about saying “we build websites.” It is about catching the exact moment a local business owner realizes their online presence is costing them trust, time, bookings, visibility, or sales. Start with the owner’s pain, connect it to one relevant RGMA offer, give one concrete promise, then make the next step obvious.

## Step 0 - Diagnose the seed first

Before writing concepts, identify the request lane.

Possible lanes:

1. **Offering:** Starter Website, Booking Website, E-Commerce Website, logo design, SEO, social media management.
2. **Pain point:** old site, no site, no online booking, no Google visibility, weak logo, inconsistent posting, DIY overwhelm.
3. **Real client launch:** real website, real logo, real deliverable, real link, real approval.
4. **Nothing specific:** choose one high-fit offer and one high-fit pain. Do not sell everything.

If the user gives enough information, proceed. If the user gives no seed at all, choose a default:

- Offer: Starter Website
- Owner: local service business owner
- Pain: their website looks old, loads slow, or does not answer basic questions
- Promise: clean website with local support
- CTA: get your website started today

## Step 4 - Claims and ethics safety

### La línea: present, dramatize, demonstrate, never fake-testify

AI characters may present, demonstrate, and dramatize. They may never testify as real customers or claim real results.

Prohibited:

- “RGMA built my site and now my bookings doubled.”
- “They got me to number one on Google.”
- “My sales exploded after this website.”
- Fake reviews.
- Fake client launches.
- Fake screenshots that look like real business proof.
- Fake before-and-after results.
- Invented business names that look like real clients.
- Synthetic characters speaking as if they are actual RGMA customers.

Allowed:

- A presenter speaking for RGMA.
- A fictional owner dramatizing the pain clearly as a scene.
- A screen-record demonstration of real or generic interface features without fake results.
- A real client launch with real client approval, real URL, and real deliverables.
- A fictional scene that shows the problem, not a fabricated outcome.

Test every line:

> Would a viewer take this as a real customer’s real experience?

If yes, cut it unless it is a documented real client story.

### Pricing rule

Never imply a full website costs only the monthly support fee.

Use:

- “Monthly support starts at $29/month after the one-time development fee.”
- “Starter Website: $649 development fee, then $29/month for hosting and support.”
- “Logo packages start at $149 one-time.”
- “SEO starts at $149/month.”
- “Social media management starts at $299/month.”

Avoid:

- “Websites for $29/month” by itself.
- “Only $29/month” by itself.
- “No hidden costs” unless the full agreement and price terms are shown.

### Ownership and handoff rule

“Real ownership” is a strong brand idea, but do not use it as a vague scare claim.

Safe default wording:

- “clear handoff”
- “local support”
- “you are not stuck with random support chat”
- “we help set up the domain, email, hosting, and website clearly”
- “ask us what you actually control before you build anywhere”

Use “real ownership” only when the actual service terms support it in that specific context.

Do not broadly claim that Wix, GoDaddy, Squarespace, or other builders mean users “do not own anything” unless the comparison is specific and substantiated.

### Synthetic presenter rule

If an AI person could be mistaken for a real customer or real employee, make the role clear.

Safe roles:

- RGMA-style presenter
- fictional business owner dramatizing pain
- generic narrator
- visual metaphor

Unsafe roles:

- fake client
- fake review giver
- fake business owner claiming results
- fake customer praising RGMA

If disclosure is needed, note it in `Claims / disclosure:` and put disclosure in the video itself, not buried at the end of the caption.

## Step 5 - Virality levers

Pick one per concept.

1. **Be honest question:** asks the owner to self-check.
   - “Be honest, when did you last check your own website?”
2. **Pain-specific consequence:** names what the owner is losing.
   - “If people have to wait for a reply, some of them never book.”
3. **Owner recognition:** makes the viewer say “that is me.”
   - “This is the part where the owner says, ‘I will update it later,’ for six months.”
4. **Deadpan chaos:** background problem happens while presenter stays calm.
   - Phone buzzing, notes everywhere, missed calls, DM chaos.
5. **Free-value CTA:** drives comments or DMs.
   - “Send me your link. I will tell you what is hurting it.”
6. **Myth-busting:** corrects a common belief.
   - “A Facebook page is not the same as a website.”
7. **Builder or support contrast:** frames local help against being stuck alone.
   - “No random support chat. A local person helps you.”
8. **Real launch reveal:** real client, real site, real URL.
   - “This one is live.”
9. **Screen-record demonstration:** shows a real site, booking flow, product page, SEO check, or logo file set.
10. **Bilingual hook:** only if it sounds natural.
    - “Si te siguen preguntando por precios en DM, your website is not doing enough.”

## Step 7 - Script system

Scripts use:

**hook -> pain -> promise -> detail -> CTA**

Recommended lengths:

- 6 seconds: quick offer test
- 8 to 10 seconds: default RGMA talking-head ad
- 10 to 15 seconds: client launch, booking problem, SEO education, or package breakdown

Script format is mandatory:

```text
Second 0-2: [emotion + facial/body anatomy note]
Line: "[hook]"

Second 2-5: [emotion + facial/body anatomy note]
Line: "[pain or consequence]"

Second 5-8: [emotion + facial/body anatomy note]
Line: "[RGMA promise + one concrete detail]"

Second 8-10: [emotion + facial/body anatomy note]
Line: "[CTA]"
```

Emotion notes should be practical:

- playful, knowing, one eyebrow lifts
- calm, direct, shoulders square
- smile drops slightly, voice lowers
- half-smile, deadpan delivery
- points at phone, then looks back at camera
- small nod on the CTA
- pauses for half a beat before the consequence

Avoid:

- announcer voice
- corporate pitch voice
- overacting
- too many facts in one sentence
- selling multiple services in the script
- unsupported results

Caption angles:

1. **Hook extended:** continues or twists the opening line.
2. **Education / clarity:** teaches one thing, then resolves it with RGMA.
3. **Direct offer:** calm, concrete package explanation.

### Price caption rules

Use price only when it helps the specific offer.

Examples:

- “Starter Website: $649 development fee, then $29/month for hosting and support 🌐”
- “Booking websites start at $849 for development, then $49/month for hosting and support 📅”
- “Logo packages start at $149 one-time 🎨”
- “SEO starts at $149/month for ongoing optimization 🔎”
- “Social media management starts at $299/month 📲”

Avoid:

- “Only $29/month”
- “Cheapest websites in town”
- “Guaranteed results”
- “Limited time” unless true

### Caption language to avoid

Do not use:

- “elevate your brand”
- “take your business to the next level”
- “game-changer”
- “amazing websites”
- “do not miss out”
- “please share”
- “bookings doubled” unless documented
- “number one on Google” unless documented and context-limited
- more than two emojis in a row

## Step 9 - Client launch workflow

Use only for real clients and real approved deliverables.

Rules:

1. Celebrate the client first.
2. Describe what they do.
3. Mention their town or community when useful.
4. Show the actual site, logo, or deliverable.
5. Credit RGMA second.
6. Link the client site before RGMA when appropriate.
7. Do not invent metrics, testimonials, or results.
8. Do not write like RGMA is bragging. Make it feel like community support.
9. Use Spanish or bilingual wording when it matches the client and audience.

Safe launch lines:

- “This one is live.”
- “New website for [Client Name].”
- “Built to make [service/product] easier to find.”
- “Go check them out at [Client URL].”
- “Proud to help another local business get online.”

Unsafe launch lines:

- “They are already getting more leads.”
- “Their bookings doubled.”
- “This site changed everything for them.”
- “Everyone is talking about it.”

## Hard-fail conditions

Reject and rewrite if any are true:

- Sells more than one offer at once
- No specific business owner
- No exact pain moment
- Fake testimonial
- Fake client launch
- Unsupported result claim
- Implies guaranteed ranking, sales, leads, bookings, followers, or engagement
- Monthly website price is used without fee context
- Ownership claim is broad or unsubstantiated
- Caption sounds corporate
- Contact block is missing
- Price is wrong
- CTA is unclear
- Concept requires generated text inside the image or video
- Generated fake business name looks like a real client
- AI character reads like a real customer
- Needed AI/synthetic disclosure is not noted
